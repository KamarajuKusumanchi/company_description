Edison International, through its subsidiaries, generates and distributes
electric power. It delivers electricity to 15 million residential, commercial,
industrial, public authorities, agricultural, and other customers across
Southern, Central, and Coastal California. The company also provides energy
solutions to commercial and industrial users. Its transmission facilities
consist of lines ranging from 55 kV to 500 kV and substations; and distribution
system consists of approximately 39,000 circuit-miles of overhead lines,
approximately 31,000 circuit-miles of underground lines, and 800 substations.
The company was founded in 1886 and is headquartered in Rosemead, California.
